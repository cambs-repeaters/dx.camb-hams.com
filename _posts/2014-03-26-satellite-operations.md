This year we have decided to improve upon our satellite operations from the Isle of Lewis, the original plan was to use a simple setup of a [Yaesu FT-817](http://www.yaesu.com/indexvs.cfm?cmd=DisplayProducts&ProdCatID=102&encProdID=06014CD0AFA0702B25B12AB4DC9C0D27) and a [Arrow satellite yagi](http://www.arrowantennas.com/arrowii/146-437.html) – although this setup works amazingly well as we proved last year on the [Isle of Mull](https://www.flickr.com/photos/m1bxf/8751606184/in/set-72157633510413437) it does have the downside of making you [stand outside in all weathers](https://www.flickr.com/photos/m1bxf/8751603412/in/set-72157633510413437), and this year we are further north!

[![photo of the Wimo X-Quads setup for testing in G1SAAs garden.](http://dx.camb-hams.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/BjBLRFSIYAEMwP1.jpg)](http://dx.camb-hams.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/BjBLRFSIYAEMwP1.jpg)

*<span style="font-size: small;">Wimo X-Quads setup for testing in G1SAAs garden</span>*

So this year we plan to trial Bob G1SAA’s satellite equipment from the island, the new station comprises [Wimo X-Quads](http://www.wimo.de/xquad-antennas_e.html) for 2m and 70cms with [SSB electronic Preamps](http://www.ssbusa.com/gaasfet.html) (SP-2000/SP-7000) and a [Yaesu G-5400B Az/EL rotator](http://www.yaesu.com/indexVS.cfm?cmd=DisplayProducts&ProdCatID=104&encProdID=79A89CEC477AA3B819EE02831F3FD5B8&DivisionID=65&isArchived=0) supported on a speaker stand.

Inside the shack we’ll be using a [Foxdelta ST-2](http://www.foxdelta.com/products/st2.htm) tracker ([LVB Clone](http://www.g6lvb.com/articles/lvbtracker/)) connected to the rotator &amp; [ICOM IC-910 transceiver](http://www.icomuk.co.uk/IC-910H/Amateur_Radio_Ham_Base_Stations), this will all be controlled using a laptop running [SatPC32](http://www.dk1tb.de/indexeng.htm).

All bar one of these are SSB/CW transponder satellites, see the [AMSAT guide on linear transponder operating](http://amsat-uk.org/beginners/how-to-work-the-ssb-satellites/) if you are unfamiliar with it, although our main focus will be SSB QSOs we can probably do CW as well however we’d recommend that you [send in a sked](mailto:skeds-2014@camb-hams.com), or [ask on hf](http://dxlite.g7vjr.org/?refresh=1&dx=gs3pye), so that we can make sure a suitable operator is available.

Of course if any other satellites with transponders become available we’ll of course endeavour to use those as well. And I’m sure you’ll still hear us using our [Yaesu FT-817s](http://www.yaesu.com/indexvs.cfm?cmd=DisplayProducts&ProdCatID=102&encProdID=06014CD0AFA0702B25B12AB4DC9C0D27) and a [Arrow satellite yagis](http://www.arrowantennas.com/arrowii/146-437.html) if the weather is nice.
